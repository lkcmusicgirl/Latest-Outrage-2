Bearing the Truth By Lauren Merkovich

When I think back to my younger years three things come to mind 1) my childhood home 2) fighting with my brothers and 3) Haribo. Every weekend my parents would go shopping. Like every other kid I would attempt to sneak my favourite snack into the cart. At that time I could not resist the need for Haribo gummy bears. I was addicted to the sugary goodness. My favourite flavour constantly changed from the clear to red bear. Haribo was my childhood candy. When I was in the fifth grade my mother decided that we would eliminate all sugars from the house hold. This was horror to my 11 year old self. This meant no more cake, soda, and worse no Haribo bears. Gummy bears had been eliminated from my life. Though I thought my world would be over I somehow managed to survive. I learned to resist my temptations and ignore the Haribo label. Flash forward July 9th 2015. Setting: inside Moody's cafeteria around 12:05pm. I am about to pay for my food when something catches my eye. I see a golden wrapper and go towards it. I pick it up and to my delight a tiny package of Haribo gummy bears are in my hands! I hadn't seen or eaten gummy bear for 6 years. This was my chance, to reunite with my childhood. Since that day I looked forward to lunch time. Eating my favourite candy once again. Yet something was off, but I could not place what exactly was the problem. As I have matured I've become conscious of this thing on the back of goods called nutrition facts. I subconsciously check the calories of the things I eat. After eating Haribo bears so many times never have I ever checked the back. Let me say I have regretted it since. I peered at the back of the half empty Haribo package, and suddenly my world stopped. I calmly looked at the calories and sugars but as my eyes continued to analyse the package I saw that my entire life had been a lie. A LIE! I was in shock. How could it be? My beloved green gummy bear was NOT green apple but rather STRAWBERRY. YES, YOU HAVE READ CORRECTLY (CHECK THE PACKET IF YOU DONT BELIEVE ME)! THE GREEN GUMMY BEAR IS STRAWBERRY FLAVOURED!! I felt betrayed. Haribo more like har-i-bul. From that moment I knew that I needed to inform the rest of the world. DO NOT BE FOOLED BY THE FRIENDLY BEAR ON THE FRONT WELCOMING YOU TO LIES. Basically the moral of the story is that ignorance is bliss. If you love something don't look at the nutrition label.
